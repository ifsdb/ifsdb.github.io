## Overview

The **HD Tile** is a self-similar plane tile generated by 9 affine maps, all with contraction
ratio $\frac{1}{3}$. Its Hausdorff dimension is $2$ and it tiles the plane by translation.

The tile is visually striking because the mix of orientations — including maps with
central symmetry ($-1$) and axis-swapping reflections ($r$) — makes the IFS look as if
its images overlap. In fact, the **Open Set Condition** holds exactly: the neighbor
graph has only 13 pairwise boundary components.

What makes the HD Tile exceptional is its **boundary dimension ≈ 1.993**, the positive
root of

$$x^7 - 12x^6 + 27x^5 + 10x^4 - 61x^3 + 20x^2 + 9x - 18 = 0.$$

This is among the highest known boundary dimensions for a self-similar tile of this type.
The boundary is an extremely "space-filling" fractal curve — it almost fills the plane
while still having zero area.

Discovered by D. Mekhontsev using the IFStile search engine.

## Structure of the Nine Maps

The attractor satisfies:

$$A = \tfrac{1}{3}\bigl(1 \cup h_1 \cup h_2 \cup h_3 \cup h_4 \cup h_5 \cup h_6 \cup h_7 \cup h_8\bigr)(A)$$

The nine maps fall into three groups by their linear part:

| Maps | Linear part | Description |
|------|------------|-------------|
| $1$ | identity | no rotation |
| $h_1, h_2, h_3$ | $-1$ (central symmetry) | 180° rotation |
| $h_4, h_5, h_6$ | identity | pure translations |
| $h_7, h_8$ | $r = \begin{pmatrix}0&1\\1&0\end{pmatrix}$ | axis-swap reflection |

The translations are:

$$h_1: [1,0], \quad h_2: [2,1], \quad h_3: [0,-1]$$

$$h_4: [-1,-1], \quad h_5: [1,1], \quad h_6: [-1,1]$$

$$h_7(x) = r\,x + [-1,0], \quad h_8(x) = r\,x + [0,1]$$

## Boundary Dimension

Computing the neighbor graph (331 intersection checks, exact rational arithmetic)
confirms the OSC holds and yields 13 pairwise boundary pieces $i_1, \ldots, i_{13}$.
Piece $i_{11}$ is a point contact (dim = 0); the remaining 12 pieces share dimension
governed by the degree-7 polynomial above, giving

$$\dim_H(\partial A) \approx 1.993 = \frac{2\log(8.935\ldots)}{\log 9}.$$

The boundary is thus nearly 2-dimensional — it almost saturates the ambient plane.
