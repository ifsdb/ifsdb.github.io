## Overview

The **Labyrinth tiling** is an aperiodic tiling with **8-fold** rotational symmetry,
related algebraically to the [Ammann–Beenker tiling](/ifs/ammann-beenker).
Both use the same 4D rational space with $s^4 = -1$ and the same expansion factor
$g = 1+s-s^3$ (the silver ratio $1+\sqrt{2}$ on the rendering eigenplane).

The tiling has **three prototile types** $A_0, A_1, A_2$ with mutual dependencies,
forming a connected GIFS system. The tile shapes have fractal boundaries arising
from the algebraic cut-and-project structure.

## Algebraic Structure

The companion matrix $s$ (AIFS: `$companion([1,0,0,0])`) satisfies $s^4 + 1 = 0$,
so $s$ represents $45°$ rotation. The expansion at eigenvalue $e^{i\pi/4}$ is:

$$
g\big|_{e^{i\pi/4}} = 1 + e^{i\pi/4} - e^{3i\pi/4} = 1 + \tfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}(1+i) + \tfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}(1-i) = 1 + \sqrt{2}
$$

The 9 unique isometry maps $q_0, \ldots, q_8$ are reassigned to $h_0, \ldots, h_{18}$
to define the three-attractor GIFS:

$$
A_0 = g^{-1}(h_0(A_0) \cup h_1\cdots h_4(A_1) \cup h_5\cdots h_8(A_2))
$$

$$
A_1 = g^{-1}(h_9(A_0) \cup h_{10}\cdots h_{12}(A_1) \cup h_{13}\cdots h_{14}(A_2))
$$

$$
A_2 = g^{-1}(h_{15}(A_0) \cup h_{16}\cdots h_{17}(A_1) \cup h_{18}(A_2))
$$
