## Overview

The **Octagonal 1225** tiling is an aperiodic 2-prototile tiling with **8-fold** rotational
symmetry. It shares the same algebraic framework as the [Ammann–Beenker](/ifs/ammann-beenker)
and [Labyrinth](/ifs/labyrinth) tilings — all three use the 4D rational space with
$s^4 = -1$ and expansion by the silver ratio $g = 1+s-s^3$.

With only **two prototile types** ($A_0$ and $A_1$) and 10 affine maps, it is the most
compact member of the 8-fold family in this file.

## Algebraic Structure

The companion $s$ (AIFS: `$companion([1,0,0,0])`) satisfies $s^4+1=0$ (rotation by $45°$).
The silver-ratio expansion $g = 1+s-s^3$ contracts by $1/(1+\sqrt{2})$ on the eigenplane of $e^{i\pi/4}$.

The GIFS has two tile types: $A_0$ (the larger tile, 7 maps) and $A_1$ (the smaller tile, 3 maps):

$$
A_0 = g^{-1}(h_3 \cup h_4)(A_1) \cup g^{-1}(h_5 \cup h_6 \cup h_7 \cup h_8 \cup h_9)(A_0)
$$

$$
A_1 = g^{-1} h_0(A_1) \cup g^{-1}(h_1 \cup h_2)(A_0)
$$
